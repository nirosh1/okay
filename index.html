ThriveToTransform

While TIME is limitless, the only constant in it is the change and this change is natural. It is orchestrated by the value added in every bit of the change, and this unfolds transformation. So, Time is transformation, from yesterday to today and tomorrow and is a symbol of evolution.

We see the past through constellations, present through our eyes, and the future through every step of we take. Limitlessly changing, this time is now and ever. So is Yugam, capturing the shades of the gone days, living in the present and creating colours to reminisce for the years to come. Yugam, from the yester years not just changed but has transformed and with this continued transformation it evolves. Let's take a break from our accelerating work and seize the moment with us this Yugam’19.

Theme

The team YUGAM has got testimonies from all over for their showmanship. The student organisers, indeed exhibit their calibre in every step of the way – from logistics to choosing the celebrities to honour various shows. Eminent personalities have been an indelible part of Yugam, who had made an impact and it is them who define yugam. The sizzling cultural events of special mention are Alexander Babu’s, stand-up comedy show, Anil Srinivasan and team’s musical treat, Team Ability unlimited, performing innovative Bharatanatyam and Sufi dances on wheel chair, the Playback singer, Chinmayi Sripada’s musical concert and a musical show by the Band, Non-Violinist Project, A musical orchestra by Stephen Devassy. Y-Talks , a mix of entertaining, inspirational and a talkative show with “Ideas Worth Spreading” as its main asset which has brought in personalities like Rajmohan, an aspiring orator trending on Youtube, “Auto” Annadurai an enterprising autodriver , Pete Draper, the co-founder of Makuta VFX (Team Baahubali). KCT’s heart always lies in giving back to the society. “Enable – Make it Possible” campaign that encouraged the differently able to showcase their skills “Make a Child Smile” Campaign providing basic requirements for 200+ children and making them smile! ‘Circle of Opportunity’ hosted a food truck called Transtrength, run by transgenders end to end, marking their inclusion!
